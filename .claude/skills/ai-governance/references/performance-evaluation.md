# Performance Evaluation

## Monitoring and Measurement

**Purpose:** Know whether the governance system and the AI system are actually performing as intended.

Determine what to monitor, how to measure it, when to check, and when to analyze results. For RecoveryLM, key metrics include: crisis detection accuracy (false negatives are critical), response safety and appropriateness, user-reported concerns, encryption and privacy control effectiveness, and whether governance objectives are being met on schedule. Keep records of monitoring results.

## Internal Audit

**Purpose:** Periodically verify that we're doing what we said we'd do, and that it's working.

Audit the governance system on a regular schedule. The audit should check conformance with our own requirements and the effectiveness of controls in practice. Plan an audit programme that considers which areas matter most, what previous audits found, and what's changed. Use auditors who can be objective (not auditing their own work). Report findings to the appropriate people and take corrective action promptly. Document everything — the programme, the criteria, the findings, and the actions taken.

## Management Review

**Purpose:** Leadership periodically steps back to evaluate the whole governance system.

At planned intervals, review whether the governance system is still suitable, adequate, and effective. Inputs to this review should include: status of actions from previous reviews, changes in context (internal and external), AI system performance trends, monitoring and audit results, progress on objectives, feedback from interested parties, risk and impact assessment results, and opportunities for improvement.

The review should result in decisions about improvement opportunities, changes needed to the governance system, and resource needs. For RecoveryLM, this is where the project lead assesses whether governance is keeping pace with development and whether priorities need to shift. Document the outcomes.
