# Leadership

## Commitment

**Purpose:** Leadership owns the governance system — it's not delegated away and forgotten.

This means actively championing responsible AI practices, ensuring governance is integrated into how the project operates (not bolted on), allocating resources to do it properly, and fostering a culture where raising AI concerns is encouraged, not punished. For RecoveryLM, this starts with the project lead setting the tone that user safety comes before feature velocity.

## AI Policy

**Purpose:** The foundational document that sets direction for everything else.

The AI policy states our principles, commitments, and boundaries for how we develop and operate AI. It should be appropriate to RecoveryLM's mission, provide a framework for setting measurable objectives, and include commitments to the needs of our interested parties. The policy must be documented and accessible — for a public repo, this means it's visible to anyone.

## Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities

**Purpose:** Make it clear who is responsible for what in the governance system.

Every governance activity needs an owner — risk assessments, policy reviews, incident response, corrective actions. For a small team, one person may wear many hats, but the responsibilities still need to be explicitly assigned and communicated so nothing falls through the cracks.
