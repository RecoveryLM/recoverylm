# Context of the Organization

## Understanding Our Context

**Purpose:** Know who we are, what we're building, and what forces shape our work.

For RecoveryLM, this means understanding:
- **Internal factors:** Our mission to support people in addiction recovery, our privacy-first architecture, the technical constraints of running AI in-browser, and our team's capabilities and limitations.
- **External factors:** Mental health regulations, AI-specific legislation (e.g. EU AI Act implications for health-adjacent systems), prohibited uses of AI, professional standards of care in addiction treatment, and the evolving landscape of responsible AI practice.
- **Our role:** We are the AI provider — we develop and operate the system. We are not a healthcare provider, but our system operates in a health-adjacent space with real consequences.

## Interested Parties

**Purpose:** Identify who is affected by or has a stake in our AI system, and what they need from us.

Key interested parties for RecoveryLM:
- **People in recovery** (primary users) — need safe, private, non-judgmental support that doesn't replace professional care
- **Support networks** (family, sponsors) — need confidence the system won't cause harm
- **Healthcare and addiction professionals** — need assurance the system complements rather than undermines treatment
- **Marginalized communities** — need equitable access and unbiased responses
- **Regulators and policymakers** — need transparency and compliance with applicable laws
- **The open-source community** — as a public repo, our governance is visible and should demonstrate genuine practice

## Scope

**Purpose:** Draw clear boundaries around what our AI management system covers.

The scope defines which AI systems, processes, and activities fall under governance. For RecoveryLM, this includes the conversational AI companion (Remmi), the crisis detection system, widget-based therapeutic exercises, and all data handling from input through response generation.

## The Management System Itself

**Purpose:** Commit to building and maintaining a living governance system — not a one-time document dump.

This means establishing governance artifacts, implementing the controls they describe, maintaining them as the system evolves, and improving them based on what we learn. The `/governance/` folder is where this lives.
