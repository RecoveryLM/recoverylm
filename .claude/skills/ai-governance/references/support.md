# Support

## Resources

**Purpose:** Make sure the governance system has what it needs to actually function.

Consider what's available internally — people, tools, time, expertise — and what needs to come from outside. For RecoveryLM, this includes access to mental health domain expertise for risk assessment, technical infrastructure for monitoring, and time allocated for governance activities alongside development work.

## Competence

**Purpose:** People working on AI systems need to know what they're doing — and what's at stake.

Determine what competencies are needed for anyone whose work affects AI system performance or governance effectiveness. Fill gaps through training, mentoring, or hiring. For RecoveryLM, this means understanding not just the technical stack but also the basics of addiction recovery, crisis intervention principles, and the ethical dimensions of AI in mental health. Keep evidence that people have the competence needed for their roles.

## Awareness

**Purpose:** Everyone contributing to the project should understand the governance context they work within.

People doing work on RecoveryLM should be aware of the AI policy, relevant objectives, how their work contributes to responsible AI outcomes, and what happens when governance requirements aren't met. A developer building a new widget should understand that their work touches a vulnerable population and that governance isn't someone else's problem.

## Communication

**Purpose:** Know what to communicate about AI governance, to whom, when, and how.

This covers both internal communication (team alignment on governance decisions) and external communication (transparency with users about AI use). For a public repo, much of this is inherently visible. Consider what interested parties need to know — users should understand what the AI can and can't do, and the community should be able to see how governance decisions are made.

## Documented Information

**Purpose:** If it isn't written down, it doesn't exist. Governance needs records.

The management system needs documented information — policies, assessments, treatment plans, audit results, corrective actions. When creating or updating documents, ensure proper identification (title, date, author), appropriate format, and review for adequacy. Control documents so they're available when needed, protected from loss or improper use, and managed through changes over time. The `/governance/` folder structure serves this purpose.
